FAQs

Common
questions.

Everything you need to know about working with Nielsen & Brown. Can't find the answer you're looking for? Get in touch.

What exactly does Nielsen & Brown do?
We help UK small and medium-sized businesses adopt AI practically, confidently, and accessibly. That means assessing where AI can genuinely help, training your team to use it, building custom tools where off-the-shelf doesn't fit, and making sure you're on the right side of the incoming UK AI Bill.
Who is Nielsen & Brown for?
We work exclusively with UK SMBs — businesses with 10 to 249 employees — across all sectors. Our sweet spot is leadership teams who know AI is important but don't have the internal expertise to know where to start or how to do it safely.
What is the UK AI Bill and should I be worried?
The UK AI Bill is forthcoming legislation that will regulate how businesses develop and use AI systems. It will affect how you document, audit, and govern your AI use. SMBs that prepare early avoid expensive scrambles when it comes into force. We help you understand your obligations now and build the right policies and processes ahead of time.
What does a typical engagement look like?
We start with a diagnostic assessment — mapping your business and identifying where AI creates real value. From there, we design and test a solution, implement it alongside your team, and then stay on as a retained partner to keep you ahead as things evolve. Most clients move through all four stages; some start with a specific need like training or compliance.
How is Nielsen & Brown different from freelance AI consultants?
A freelancer gives you one person with one specialism. Nielsen & Brown gives you a structured, multi-disciplinary team covering strategy, training, and software development — so you don't need to co-ordinate multiple suppliers or worry about the handoffs between them. We also bring formal processes, CPD-accredited training, and compliance expertise that individual contractors typically can't offer.
Do you work with businesses outside London?
Yes — we work with SMBs across the whole of the UK. We operate remote-first, so geography isn't a barrier. Where on-site work is needed (for example, in-person training workshops), we can travel.
